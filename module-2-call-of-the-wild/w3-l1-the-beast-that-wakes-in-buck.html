The Beast That Wakes in Buck

📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
⌚ ~12 min
For two chapters something wild has been stirring in Buck. In Chapter 3 it finally breaks loose. London calls it “the dominant primordial beast.” What is it, and what wakes it up?
You will read the first half of Chapter 3 and track a rivalry. Buck and Spitz have hated each other since Curly died. This chapter turns that hatred into open war, and it shows you the exact moment the beast in Buck takes over.
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I will be able to explain what London means by “the dominant primordial beast” and how it grows in Buck.
2
I will be able to trace the rivalry between Buck and Spitz to the moment it becomes a fight to the death.
3
I will be able to point to details that show Buck is changing from the inside, not just surviving the cold.

▶
The Call of the Wild, Chapter 3, The Dominant Primordial Beast (first half, through the war between Buck and Spitz). [Teacher: link the Chapter 3 PDF in Canvas Files here]
▶
Your Call of the Wild Reading Journal on OneDrive.
💡 THE ONE IDEA TO HOLD ONTO
The “dominant primordial beast” is the old, wild, take-charge nature of Buck’s wolf ancestors. London says it grows in Buck, but in secret, held down by his “newborn cunning.” Watch how the rivalry with Spitz pushes it from hidden to open, step by step.
STEP 1 · HIDDEN
Buck avoids fights. The beast is “a secret growth,” held back by caution.
⬇️
STEP 2 · THE SPARK
Spitz steals Buck’s warm nest at Lake Le Barge. “The beast in him roared.”
⬇️
STEP 3 · OPEN WAR
After Spitz attacks him during the mad-dog chase, “From then on it was war between them.”
📚 PART 1 · READ & NOTICE: BEFORE YOU READ
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
Buck has been holding back for two chapters. Why might a smart, dangerous animal choose to wait instead of attacking right away? (No wrong answer. Just check in with yourself.)
📚 PART 2 · READ & NOTICE: READ CHAPTER 3, FIRST HALF

Read from the start of Chapter 3 through the line “From then on it was war between them.” The chapter opens by naming the beast: it “was strong in Buck, and under the fierce conditions of trail life it grew and grew.” At a bleak camp on Lake Le Barge, Spitz takes Buck’s warm nest, and Buck springs at him for the first time. Before the fight can finish, a pack of starving huskies storms the camp. Later, when Buck is exhausted from a chase, Spitz attacks him from the side, and the long contest finally turns into open war.

💡 KEEP THIS IN MIND
A rivalry is a contest between two who each want the same thing. Here, both Buck and Spitz want to lead the team. London also names a force he calls pride: “that nameless, incomprehensible pride of the trail and trace.” That pride is part of why Buck wants the fight, not just the cold or the hunger.
⚡ TRY IT · WHAT DRIVES THE RIVALRY?

Each statement below comes from your reading. Decide whether it is something London states directly about the rivalry, or a conclusion you build from the details. Decide in your head before you choose.

1. Spitz feels his position as lead-dog is threatened by Buck.
2. The growing beast in Buck is what finally pushes him to challenge Spitz.
3. Outside events keep interrupting the fight before it can settle.
4. Spitz stealing Buck’s warm nest is what makes Buck attack the first time.
📚 PART 4 · READ & NOTICE: THE BEAST IN BUCK

London says Buck has a “newborn cunning” that gives him “poise and control.” He no longer fights wildly, the way he did against the man in the red sweater. The beast is awake, but Buck now uses his head. Notice how the chapter shows both: raw instinct and cold calculation, side by side.

✏️ READING JOURNAL · ADD THIS TO THE WEEK 3 SECTION

Write a few sentences for each question. For each one, point to a detail you can find in the first half of Chapter 3.

  1. What single event finally makes Buck attack Spitz for the first time? Name the detail, then explain what it shows about Buck.
  2. London says the beast in Buck is “a secret growth” with “poise and control.” How is this Buck different from the Buck who first fought the man with the club in Chapter 1?
  3. By the end of this section, why does Buck want the fight with Spitz? Point to the “pride of the trail” passage and explain it in your own words.
⚠️ WORTH KNOWING · WHERE A TOOL STOPS AND YOU START
An AI chatbot can tell you that Buck and Spitz are enemies. It cannot do the work you are doing now: finding the exact moment the rivalry turns, and tying it to a detail you can point to. That skill, locating the evidence yourself, is what your whole argument essay will rest on. Build it now while the stakes are small.

💪 BEFORE YOU GO

The beast in Buck did not appear out of nowhere. London built it across three chapters, then let one stolen nest set it loose. Next lesson you finish the chapter and watch the fight itself, and you will study how London makes the frozen wild feel like a living thing.
