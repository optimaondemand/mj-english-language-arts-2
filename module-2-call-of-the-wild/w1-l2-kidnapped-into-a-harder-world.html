📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
⌚ ~12 min
A gardener’s helper loops a rope around Buck’s neck and sells him. Two days later Buck has not eaten, has not drunk, and has stopped being a comfortable king. What did the new world do to him so fast?
You will read the kidnapping pages of Chapter 1. You will track how each new place Buck is dragged through, the crate, the baggage car, the express car, presses on him and starts to change him. This is the heart of the module question: the setting is remaking the character.
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I will be able to retell the events of Buck’s kidnapping in order, from Manuel’s betrayal to the express car.
2
I will be able to point to specific lines where the harsh new setting changes how Buck feels and acts.
3
I will be able to explain how a change of setting can drive a change in a character.

💡 THE ONE IDEA TO HOLD ONTO
Buck does not change all at once. Each new place takes a little more of his old self. Read this like a staircase going down: with every step, the setting gets harsher and Buck gets harder.
STEP 1 · THE ORCHARD AT NIGHT
Buck trusts Manuel and accepts the rope “with quiet dignity.” He is still the trusting king.
⬇️
STEP 2 · THE BAGGAGE CAR
The rope chokes him. He bites the man’s hand. The trust is gone; rage takes its place.
⬇️
STEP 3 · TWO DAYS IN THE EXPRESS CAR
No food, no water, only teasing. London says Buck was “metamorphosed into a raging fiend,” so changed the Judge would not know him.
📚 PART 1 · READ & NOTICE: BEFORE YOU READ
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
When Buck first feels the rope tighten, his trust turns to fury. Before you read, what do you expect two days of hunger and rough handling will do to a proud animal? (No wrong answer.)
📚 PART 2 · READ & NOTICE: READ THE KIDNAPPING PAGES

Read from the night of Manuel’s treachery to the end of the express-car ride. Manuel, a gardener’s helper who gambles, secretly leads Buck off the estate and hands him to a stranger for money. When the rope tightens, Buck fights, but he is choked into a faint and thrown in a baggage car. He bites the man, is choked again, and is filed out of his brass collar and crated up. For two days and nights the express car drags him north. He neither eats nor drinks, and the messengers tease him through the bars.

Watch London’s word choices. Buck is no longer “king.” He is a “kidnapped king,” then a creature whose eyes turn “blood-shot,” then one “metamorphosed into a raging fiend.” The setting did this. The same dog, in a worse place, becomes a different dog.

💡 KEEP THIS IN MIND
A dynamic character is one who changes over the course of a story. Buck is the clearest kind: you can almost measure his change page by page. When you read, ask not just “what happened” but “what did this place do to him?”
⚡ TRY IT · HOW IS BUCK CHANGING?

Each line below describes a moment in the kidnapping. Decide whether it shows Buck’s OLD trusting self or his NEW, hardening self. Decide in your head before you choose.

1. Buck takes the rope “with quiet dignity,” trusting the men he knows.
2. When the rope chokes him, Buck springs at the man in a rage.
3. For two days Buck refuses food and water and stores up wrath.
4. Each time the shed door opens, Buck hopes to see the Judge or the boys.
📚 PART 4 · READ & NOTICE: WRITE ABOUT WHAT YOU READ

London changes the setting around Buck, and Buck changes inside it. Capture how that works in your own words.

✏️ READING JOURNAL · ADD THIS TO THE WEEK 1 SECTION

Write a few sentences for each question. Point to a detail from the reading whenever you can.

  1. How does Buck’s behavior change between the orchard at night and the end of the express-car ride? Name one detail from each moment.
  2. London writes that Buck was “metamorphosed into a raging fiend.” What does that line tell you, and what caused the change?
  3. Pick one new place Buck is dragged through (the crate, the baggage car, the express car). Explain how that place pushes Buck further from the dog he was at home.
⚠️ WORTH KNOWING · WHERE A TOOL STOPS AND YOU START
An AI chatbot can list the events of the kidnapping for you in seconds. It cannot do the move you just practiced: reading a line like “metamorphosed into a raging fiend” and explaining what the setting is doing to the character. Tracing cause and effect in a text is your work, and it is the skill the whole module is building toward.
