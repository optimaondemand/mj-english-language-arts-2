Lesson 2.03 · Friday Workshop
The Law of the Club
Short Lesson
📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
⌚ ~10 min
A stout man in a red sweater opens Buck’s crate with a club in his hand. By the end of the beating, Buck has learned a lesson he keeps for the rest of his life. What is it?
This is a short Friday lesson. You will finish Chapter 1: the fight with the man in the red sweater, the lesson Buck learns, his sale to Perrault and François, and his first snow. Then you will do your Quick Write and the Friday Check-up.
📋 WHAT YOU NEED FOR THIS LESSON
▶
The Call of the Wild, Chapter 1, the rest of the chapter: from the man in the red sweater to Buck’s first snow. [Teacher: link the Chapter 1 PDF in Canvas Files here]
▶
Your Call of the Wild Reading Journal on OneDrive.
📚 PART 1 · READ & NOTICE: BEFORE YOU READ
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
Buck has spent two days storing up rage. Now a man opens his crate. What do you think Buck does first? (No wrong answer.)
📚 PART 2 · READ & NOTICE: FINISH CHAPTER 1

Read to the end of the chapter. In a high-walled yard in Seattle, the man in the red sweater pries open Buck’s crate and meets each charge with the club. A dozen times Buck rushes; a dozen times the club smashes him down, until he is “knocked utterly senseless.” When he comes to, he is beaten but, London says, “not broken.” He drinks water and eats from the man’s hand. London tells us this club is “a revelation”: Buck’s introduction to “the reign of primitive law.” A man with a club is a master to obey. Then a French-Canadian named Perrault buys Buck, and as the ship pulls away Buck sees the last of the warm Southland. At the end, his feet sink into something white and cold: his first snow.

💡 THE ONE IDEA TO HOLD ONTO
London turns the beating into a turning point. Watch the three beats of the lesson Buck learns and keeps for life.
🪓
The blow
A man with a club can beat him. Buck cannot win by fury alone.
🧠
The lesson
“A man with a club was a lawgiver, a master to be obeyed.”
🦊
The survivor
Beaten but not broken, Buck keeps his old cunning and learns to wait.
⚡ TRY IT · DID YOU CATCH IT?

Answer each in your head, then click to check.

1. After the beating, what is true of Buck?
2. What does the club come to mean to Buck?
3. How does Chapter 1 end?
📚 PART 4 · READ & NOTICE: WRITE ABOUT WHAT YOU READ
✏️ READING JOURNAL · ADD THIS TO THE WEEK 1 SECTION

Write a few sentences for each question. Point to a detail from the reading whenever you can.

  1. London says Buck was “beaten…but he was not broken.” What is the difference between being beaten and being broken, and why does it matter for the rest of the book?
  2. The chapter is titled “Into the Primitive.” Using what you have read this week, explain what “the primitive” Buck has entered is like.
