📋 HOW TO USE THIS PAGE
Your Module 2 Final Project has two components, and they are submitted on two separate pages in the Module Wrappers and Project area. Work down each list below and check off each item as you confirm it. When every box is checked, you are ready to submit. Read the project rubric once more so you know what earns full credit.
✏️ COMPONENT 1 · ARGUMENTATIVE ESSAY
Submitted as a Word document on the CotW Final Project — Written (Argumentative Essay) page.
☐
My thesis answers the project question about Buck’s transformation in one clear sentence.
☐
I give two pieces of evidence from the novel, each a real detail I could point to in the text, with at least one from the final chapter.
☐
After each piece of evidence I wrote an explanation that says how it supports my thesis, in my own words.
☐
I name a counterclaim fairly, then respond to it with a reason my reading still holds.
☐
My conclusion restates the point in fresh words and says what the novel argues about civilization and the wild.
☐
The doc is saved in my OneDrive course folder and submitted on the CotW Final Project — Written page.
🎨 COMPONENT 2 · CREATIVE AND VR TRANSFORMATION
Submitted on the CotW Final Project — Creative + VR page.
☐
My creative artifact (my choice of form) shows Buck’s before and after so the transformation is clear.
☐
My VR transformation scene stages the civilized world and the wild side by side, with a label on each side, and I captured it as a short video or screenshot. If I could not capture it inside VR, I wrote a short description instead.
☐
My reflection is 200 to 250 words and explains what the change shows about the theme, not just what I made.
☐
All three pieces are uploaded and submitted on the CotW Final Project — Creative + VR page.
✅ TWO LAST CHECKS
☐
I read the project rubric and checked my work against all of its criteria, including intellectual honesty: every idea is mine or is clearly the novel’s, and any quotation is copied exactly.
☐
Both components are submitted by the end of Week 6.
🎉
You have finished Module 2.
You followed Buck from a warm California yard all the way to the head of the wolf pack, and you turned what you noticed into a real argument and a scene you built with your own hands. That is serious work. Submit with confidence, and carry one question forward: when something deep inside us answers a call, do we lose ourselves, or finally become who we were meant to be?
