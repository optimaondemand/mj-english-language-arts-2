📖 WHAT THIS SESSION IS FOR
Show the transformation, do not just describe it.
You will plan the creative half of your project. First a creative artifact of your choice that shows Buck’s change from pampered house dog to creature of the wild. Then a VR transformation scene that reuses the Call of the Wild VR work you built earlier in the module. Then a short reflection that ties it to the theme. By the end you will know exactly what to make for the CotW Final Project — Creative + VR page. This session is practice. It is not graded.
📋 WHAT YOU NEED OPEN
▶
Your saved Call of the Wild VR scenes from across the module: your early staging of Buck’s warm Santa Clara home, and your later wild scenes such as the law of club and fang and the Northland trail.
▶
Your VR headset and whatever you will use for the creative artifact: paper and supplies, a drawing or design app, or a document.
▶
The CotW Final Project — Creative + VR page (open it in Canvas in the Module Wrappers and Project area).
💡 THE TRANSFORMATION AT THE HEART OF YOUR PROJECT
Your creative work shows one idea: the same Buck stands at the start and at the end of the book, but everything about him has changed. Your artifact and your VR scene should make a viewer feel that change.
BEFORE (Chapter 1, civilization)
The pampered house dog
Buck rules a warm, sun-kissed California estate. He is comfortable, trusting, and safe. He has never known hunger or the club.
➞
AFTER (Chapter 7, the wild)
The leader of the pack
Buck runs free at the head of the wolves, “leaping gigantic above his fellows,” singing “a song of the younger world.” The tame dog is gone.
Same dog, two worlds. That change is your subject. A strong project makes a viewer feel both the comfort he leaves and the wild he answers.
📚 STEP 1 · CHOOSE YOUR CREATIVE ARTIFACT

You choose the form. Whatever you pick, it must show Buck’s transformation, the before and the after, so the change is clear to a viewer.

Option What it is Good if you like to
Before-and-after artworkA split image or two panels: Buck the house dog, then Buck the pack leader.draw or design.
Comic stripA short strip that moves Buck through three or four stages of his change, with captions.tell a story in panels.
Diorama or modelA two-sided model: the warm estate on one side, the snowy wild on the other.build with your hands.
Found-poem or soundtrackA poem built from the book’s own words, or a short playlist with a one-line note on why each song fits a stage of Buck’s change.work with words or music.
🧠 STEP 2 · STAGE THE VR TRANSFORMATION SCENE

You already built both worlds in your earlier VR work this module. Now put them in one scene so a viewer feels the change. Put on your VR headset, open the virtual environment for this course, and choose any setting you like with open ground.

1
On one side, rebuild Buck’s civilized world from your earlier scene: warm, calm objects for the Santa Clara estate, with a single figure for the comfortable house dog.
2
On the other side, rebuild the wild: cold, open objects for the Northland forest, with a pack of figures and Buck out in front of them.
3
Place a path or a row of objects between the two sides to show the journey, and add two labels or sticky notes, one per side. On each, write one sentence about who Buck is in that world.
4
Stand where both sides are in view, then record a short video or take a screenshot that captures the change. If you cannot capture inside VR, write a short description of what your scene shows instead.
📚 PLAN YOUR REFLECTION

Your reflection is 200 to 250 words. It should explain what your artifact and VR scene show about Buck’s transformation and the theme, not just describe what you made.

⚡ TRY IT · IS THIS REFLECTION SENTENCE STRONG OR WEAK?

For each sentence, decide whether it explains an idea about Buck’s change (strong), only describes what is in the scene (weak), or sits in between. Decide first, then choose.

1. “Putting the warm estate on one side and the wolf pack on the other shows that the wild did not break Buck. It freed the strong creature the comfortable house never asked him to be.”
2. “On the left I put a house and a sunny yard, and on the right I put snow, trees, and five gray wolves.”
3. “I put Buck in front of the pack instead of inside it, which felt important to me.”
4. “My first label calls Buck ‘a king who has never been afraid,’ and my second calls him ‘a creature who answers a call older than people,’ which is how I show what the wild wakes up in him.”
➡️ WHAT YOU’LL DO NEXT
1
Make the three pieces
Build your creative artifact, stage and capture your VR transformation scene, and write your 200 to 250 word reflection. Submit them on the CotW Final Project — Creative + VR page.
2
Run the Submission Checklist
Before you turn anything in, open the Submission Checklist page and confirm every piece of both components is ready.
💪 BEFORE YOU GO

You built both worlds earlier in the module. Now you set them side by side so a viewer feels the change, and you say in your own words what that change shows. Make the transformation clear, and let your reflection reach the idea, not just the picture.
