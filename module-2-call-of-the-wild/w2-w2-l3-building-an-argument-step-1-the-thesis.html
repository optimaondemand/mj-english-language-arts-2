Building an Argument, Step 1: The Thesis Statement
Short lesson
📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
⌚ ~10 min
All module long you are building one argument, step by step, and turning it into an essay. This week is Step 1: the thesis statement. A thesis is the single clear claim your whole essay will defend. Today you will learn what makes a thesis strong, then write your own about how Buck is changing.
💡 WHERE THIS STEP FITS
Your essay is built one step per week. You are on the first step now.
STEP 1 · THIS WEEK
Thesis: the one claim you will defend.
STEP 2 · NEXT WEEK
Evidence and explanation: prove it with the text.
STEP 3 · LATER
Counterclaim and conclusion: answer the other side and close.
💡 WHAT A THESIS NEEDS
A thesis is not a fact, and it is not a question. It is a claim someone could disagree with, that you can back up. A strong one has three parts.
🎯
A clear claim
It takes a side. Someone could argue the opposite.
🔎
Specific, not vague
It names exactly what you mean, not just “Buck changes.”
⚖️
Provable from the text
You can point to moments in the book that support it.
📚 A WORKED EXAMPLE: A CLAIM ABOUT HOW BUCK IS CHANGING

Watch a thesis get built from a weak first try.

TOO WEAK
“Buck changes a lot in Chapter 2.”
True, but no one would argue with it, and it does not say how or what it means.
GETTING THERE
“Buck becomes tougher in the Northland.”
More specific, but it only names a gain. The interesting argument is whether the change is good.
STRONG THESIS
“In Chapter 2, Buck grows stronger and smarter, but he gains those skills by losing the trust and fairness of his old self.”
It takes a side (the change is both a gain and a loss), it is specific, and you can point to the text to prove it.
⚡ TRY IT · IS IT A THESIS?

For each statement, decide whether it works as a thesis (a claim you could defend) or not. Decide in your head first.

1. “Buck is harnessed to a sled in Chapter 2.”
2. “Buck’s new ability to steal food shows survival mattering more than right and wrong.”
3. “Does Buck change for the better or the worse?”
4. “The death of Curly is the moment Buck stops trusting and starts surviving.”
⚠️ WORTH KNOWING · WHERE A TOOL STOPS AND YOU START
An AI chatbot can produce a tidy-sounding thesis in seconds. But a thesis is your claim, the position you have decided to defend. If you let a tool pick it, you spend the rest of the essay arguing for an idea you do not actually hold, and it shows. Decide what you think first. The skill of staking out a claim you can defend is exactly what this module builds.

💪 BEFORE YOU GO

A good essay is just one claim, defended well. Get the claim right first, and everything you write after it has a job to do. Your thesis this week is the seed of the whole essay.
