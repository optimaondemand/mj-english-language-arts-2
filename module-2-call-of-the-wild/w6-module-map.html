🎯 THE MODULE QUESTION, ONE LAST TIME
What transforms Buck from a pampered house dog into a creature of the wild, and what does his change reveal about instinct, survival, and the pull between civilization and the wild?
In Chapter 7 the pull finally wins. John Thornton, the one tie Buck has left to the world of men, is gone, and Buck runs with the wolf pack. This week you decide what you think that transformation means, and you argue it.

📤 WHAT IS DUE THIS WEEK
The Week 6 Reading Journal entry is due after Day 1. Both parts of the Module 2 Final Project are due by the end of the week. The two deliverable pages live in Canvas in the Module Wrappers and Project area. Open each one and follow its directions:
CotW Final Project — Written (Argumentative Essay): your full argumentative essay answering the project question about Buck’s transformation, with a thesis, evidence and explanation, a counterclaim, and a conclusion. Submitted as a Word document.
CotW Final Project — Creative + VR: your creative artifact (your choice), your VR transformation scene as a video or screenshot, and your 200 to 250 word reflection.
Both components are scored with the same rubric used across the year: Understanding of the text, Quality of reasoning and evidence, Clarity and precision, and Intellectual honesty. Read it before you submit.
📁 PULL THESE TOGETHER BEFORE YOU START THE PROJECT
You already built every piece of the argument. Open your OneDrive course folder and your saved VR work and have these ready: Quick Write 2 (your thesis and reason), Quick Write 3 (an evidence paragraph), Quick Write 4 (a counterclaim and conclusion), Quick Write 5 (your outline and intro draft), and your earlier Call of the Wild VR scenes from across the module. The two work-session lessons this week walk you through using them.
