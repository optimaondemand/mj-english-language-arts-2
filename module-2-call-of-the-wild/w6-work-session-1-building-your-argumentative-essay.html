💡 THE SHAPE OF AN ARGUMENTATIVE ESSAY
An argument is more than an opinion. It states a claim, backs it with evidence you explain, deals fairly with the other side, and closes. Here is the shape your essay takes, and which Quick Write each part grows from. The project question is: Does Buck’s transformation set him free, or destroy who he was?
PART 1 · THESIS (from Quick Write 2, polished in Quick Write 5)
State your answer to the project question in one clear sentence. Name what Buck becomes and what you say his change means.
PART 2 · EVIDENCE + EXPLANATION, FIRST PIECE (from Quick Write 3)
Give one detail from the novel. Then explain in your own words how it supports your thesis. The explanation does the real work.
PART 3 · EVIDENCE + EXPLANATION, SECOND PIECE (from Quick Write 3 plus Chapter 7)
Give a second detail, ideally one from the final chapter, and explain how it adds to your point. Two pieces are stronger than one.
PART 4 · COUNTERCLAIM + RESPONSE (from Quick Write 4)
Name the strongest point on the other side fairly, then explain why your reading still holds. This is what makes it an argument and not just an opinion.
PART 5 · CONCLUSION (from Quick Write 4 and Quick Write 5)
Restate your point in new words and say what the novel argues about the pull between civilization and the wild. Do not just repeat the thesis.
📚 READ & NOTICE: A WORKED EXAMPLE

Here is a short argument that takes the “set him free” side. Notice how each labeled part matches the shape above. Do not copy this position or these sentences. It is a model for the moves, not an answer to borrow. Your essay should be your own reading, in full paragraphs.

THESIS Buck’s transformation sets him free, because the wild lets him become the strong, instinct-driven creature he was always meant to be.

EVIDENCE 1 When Buck first runs with the wild brother, he is “wildly glad... running free in the open, the unpacked earth underfoot.” (Then the explanation:) London does not write this as a fall but as joy, which suggests the wild gives Buck a happiness the harness never could.

EVIDENCE 2 On the last page Buck runs “at the head of the pack... leaping gigantic above his fellows.” (Then the explanation:) He is not destroyed but fulfilled. He becomes a leader, larger and freer than any tame dog could be.

COUNTERCLAIM A reader might say the change destroys Buck, since the loving dog becomes a killer the Yeehats fear as an Evil Spirit. (Then the response:) But London frames that fierceness as power, not ruin. Buck loses his old life, yet he gains his truest self, so the cost is real but it is not destruction.

CONCLUSION By letting Buck answer the call, London argues that the wild is not the death of who we are but the place where a deep, ancient self can finally live.

See how each piece of evidence is followed by a sentence that explains what it shows. The counterclaim is named honestly, then answered. That fairness is what earns credit on the Intellectual honesty criterion.

⚡ TRY IT · NAME EACH PART OF THE ARGUMENT

Below are sentences from an argumentative essay about Buck, out of order. For each one, decide which part of the shape it is. Decide in your head first, then choose.

1. “London writes this moment as joy, not as a fall, which suggests the wild gives Buck something the sled team never could.”
2. “A reader might argue that the transformation destroys Buck, since the dog who loved Thornton becomes a killer the Yeehats fear.”
3. “After Thornton dies, the narrator says, ‘The last tie was broken. Man and the claims of man no longer bound him.’”
4. “In the end, London argues that the wild is not where Buck is lost but where his oldest, truest self is finally set loose.”
🧠 NOW BUILD YOUR ESSAY

Open your four Quick Writes and the CotW Final Project — Written page. In the doc, build your essay part by part using your own reading and your own details:

Introduction and thesis: start from your Quick Write 5 intro. Make sure your thesis answers the project question in one clear sentence.
Evidence 1 and its explanation: use your strongest detail from Quick Write 3, then write the sentence that says what it proves about Buck’s change.
Evidence 2 and its explanation: add a second detail, ideally from Chapter 7, and explain it the same way.
Counterclaim and response: bring in your Quick Write 4 counterclaim, state it fairly, then give your reason it does not win.
Conclusion: use Quick Writes 4 and 5 to restate your point in fresh words and name what the novel argues about civilization and the wild.
