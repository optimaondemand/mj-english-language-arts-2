Grammar 2.07
Semicolons and Colons: Two Marks That Join
Lesson
📝 TODAY'S FOCUS
⌚ ~12 min
A semicolon and a colon are both stronger than a comma, but they do different jobs. A semicolon joins two complete sentences that belong together. A colon says “here it comes” and points to a list or an explanation. Jack London uses both all through Chapters 4 and 5. Today you will learn the one rule behind each.
📚 THIS WEEK'S SENTENCE (from Chapter 5)
“On the next day Koona went, and but five of them remained: Joe, Pike, Sol-leks, Teek, and Buck.”
A shortened version of London’s line as the starving team is whittled down.
⚡ WARM-UP
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
In this week’s sentence, what is the mark doing right before the list of dogs? (No wrong answer. Just notice it.)
💡 THE ONE RULE TO REMEMBER
Both marks are stronger than a comma. The trick is what sits on each side.
;
SEMICOLON
Joins two complete sentences that are closely related. A complete sentence sits on each side. You could swap in a period and both halves would still stand alone.
Buck was very tired; he kept his mates up to their work.
:
COLON
Follows a complete sentence and points ahead to a list or an explanation. It means “here is what I mean.”
Five of them remained: Joe, Pike, Sol-leks, Teek, and Buck.
📚 HOW TO CHOOSE: TEST EACH SIDE

Before you place either mark, look at what comes after it.

  1. Is a complete sentence coming after the mark? If yes, and it is closely tied to the sentence before, use a semicolon.
  2. Is a list or an explanation coming after the mark? If yes, and a complete sentence comes before it, use a colon.
  3. Check the front, too. Both marks need a complete sentence on the left. “Five of them remained” is a complete sentence, so the colon is correct.
Label the week’s sentence:
but five of them remained
complete sentence (independent clause) :
colon, points to the list Joe, Pike, Sol-leks, Teek, and Buck.
the list

A common slip is using a colon when no complete sentence comes first. “The dogs that remained were: Joe, Pike…” is wrong, because “The dogs that remained were” cannot stand alone before the colon.
