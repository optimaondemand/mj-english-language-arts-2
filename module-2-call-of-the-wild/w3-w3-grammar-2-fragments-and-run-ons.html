Grammar 2.06
Fragments and Run-ons: Too Little and Too Much
Lesson
📝 TODAY'S FOCUS
⌚ ~12 min
A complete sentence holds exactly one complete thought, with the right punctuation. A fragment has too little: it is missing part of the thought. A run-on has too much: two complete thoughts crammed together. Today you will spot both and fix them, using the clause language from yesterday.
📚 THIS WEEK'S SENTENCE (from Chapter 3)
“The beast in him roared.”
The instant Buck springs at Spitz over the stolen nest. Short, but a complete thought.
⚡ WARM-UP
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
“The beast in him roared” is only five words. Can a sentence that short still be complete? (No wrong answer.)
💡 THE ONE RULE TO REMEMBER
A complete sentence is one independent clause, punctuated on its own. Trouble comes from having too little or too much.
⏱️
Fragment: too little
Not a complete thought. Often a dependent clause left on its own.
“Because the beast in him roared.”
✅
Complete: just right
One complete thought, punctuated alone.
“The beast in him roared.”
💧
Run-on: too much
Two complete thoughts jammed together with no break.
“The beast in him roared he sprang at Spitz.”
📚 HOW TO FIX EACH ONE

Test the group of words. Does it have a subject, a verb, and one finished thought, with nothing extra crammed on?

  1. Fix a fragment by adding what is missing, usually by joining it to a complete thought. “Because the beast in him roared” becomes “Because the beast in him roared, Buck sprang at Spitz.”
  2. Fix a run-on by separating the two thoughts. You have three good tools: a period, a semicolon, or a comma plus a conjunction (and, but, so, for).
Three ways to fix the run-on “The beast in him roared he sprang at Spitz”:
The beast in him roared. period The beast in him roared. He sprang at Spitz.
semicolon The beast in him roared; he sprang at Spitz.
comma + conjunction The beast in him roared, and he sprang at Spitz.

Notice the trap: a comma alone (“The beast in him roared, he sprang at Spitz”) is not enough. A comma needs a conjunction to join two complete thoughts.
