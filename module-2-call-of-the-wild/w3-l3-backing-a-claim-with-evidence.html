📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
⌚ ~10 min
Two weeks ago you wrote a claim about Buck. A claim by itself is just an opinion. Today you learn how to make a reader believe it.
You are building one argument piece by piece. The next layer is evidence and explanation: back your claim with a real quote from the book, then explain in your own words why that quote proves your point. Today’s workshop sets up Quick Write 3, your evidence paragraph.

2
I will be able to introduce a quote smoothly and punctuate it correctly.
3
I will be able to write an explanation that connects the quote back to my claim.

▶
Your Call of the Wild Reading Journal on OneDrive, including the thesis you wrote in Week 2 and your Chapter 3 notes.
▶
Your copy of Chapter 3, to find an exact quote.
💡 THE THREE-PART EVIDENCE PARAGRAPH
An evidence paragraph stacks three moves in order. Skip the third and a reader is left asking “so what?”
1 · CLAIM
State your point in one sentence. By Chapter 3, the wild side of Buck has taken charge.
⬇️
2 · EVIDENCE (a real quote)
Introduce and quote the book. When Spitz takes his nest, London writes, “The beast in him roared.”
⬇️
3 · EXPLANATION (the so-what)
Tie the quote to the claim. Buck no longer pauses to think. The word “roared” shows instinct, not caution, is now in control.
📚 PART 1 · READ & NOTICE: WHY THE QUOTE MATTERS
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
Imagine a friend says “Buck is becoming wild,” but gives no proof from the book. Would you be convinced? (No wrong answer.)
⚡ TRY IT · WHICH QUOTE PROVES THE CLAIM?

For each claim, pick the quote from Chapter 3 that best supports it. A good quote points straight at the claim, not just near it. Decide first, then click.

Claim: Buck wins because he fights with his head, not just instinct.
Claim: Buck wants to lead the team because of pride, not just survival.
Claim: By the end of Chapter 3, the wild has fully taken hold of Buck.
🎯 FRIDAY WORKSHOP · PUTTING IT TOGETHER

This week you tracked the rivalry, studied figurative language, and learned to fix fragments and run-ons so your sentences are clean. Now you turn those skills toward your argument. In Quick Write 3 you will build one full evidence paragraph: claim, quote, explanation.

✏️ READING JOURNAL · ADD THIS TO THE WEEK 3 SECTION

Look back at the thesis you wrote in Week 2. Write one claim that supports it, about how Buck is changing. Then hunt through Chapter 3 and copy out one exact quote that proves your claim. Write the page or paragraph it came from next to it. You will use both in Quick Write 3.

💪 BEFORE YOU GO

A claim tells a reader what you think. A quote shows them it is true. An explanation tells them why it matters. Stack all three and your argument stands up on its own, the way Buck finally stood at the end of the fight.
