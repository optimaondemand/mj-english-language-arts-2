📖 A HEAVIER READING WEEK
This week you read all of Part IV, Chapters 1 through 8. That is the longest stretch of reading in the module, about 120 minutes in all. To keep the week balanced, there are only two literature lessons and one VR activity, and the lessons are shorter than usual. Spread the reading across the week using the day-by-day plan below.
🎯 THIS WEEK'S QUESTION
Gulliver lands in a country where calm, reasoning horses rule and filthy, greedy creatures called Yahoos are the beasts. The trouble is, the Yahoos look exactly like him. By putting these two societies side by side, what is Swift saying about human nature?

📝 THE PROJECT IS NEAR · THIS WEEK FEEDS IT
Your Quick Write 6 is one evidence paragraph: a claim, one quotation from Part IV, and your explanation. Keep it. Next week you start building the full Module 1 project argument, and this paragraph drops right in. Your VR activity this week also gives you a contrast you can use.
📖 ONGOING ALL MODULE
Keep your Gulliver’s Travels Reading Journal open as you read Chapters 1 through 8. Add this week’s vocabulary, and jot two lists as you go: what makes the Houyhnhnms admirable, and what makes the Yahoos repulsive. You will use both lists in your lessons and your Quick Write. Your teacher collects the Journal at the module checkpoints, not every week.
