🎯 THIS WEEK'S QUESTION
Gulliver spends hours bragging to a giant king about England. The king listens, asks hard questions, and reaches a judgment Gulliver never expected. What is Swift really saying about human pride, and could a country be run a different way?

💬 THIS WEEK IN CANVAS
Watch your Canvas modules for the Module Discussion this week. It asks what Swift is saying about human nature, and it is the one place this module where you talk through your ideas with classmates. Post your own response, then read and reply to at least one other student.
📝 LOOKING AHEAD · THE PROJECT STARTS HERE
Your Quick Write 5 this week is the first draft of your Module 1 project argument. You will write a thesis statement and one reason answering which voyage, Lilliput or Brobdingnag, better reveals Swift’s view of humanity. Keep it. You will build on it in the coming weeks, and both VR activities this week give you evidence to use.
📖 ONGOING ALL MODULE
Keep your Gulliver’s Travels Reading Journal open as you read Chapters 6 through 8. Add this week’s vocabulary, note the king’s questions and his final judgment of humankind, and record how Gulliver finally leaves Brobdingnag. Your teacher collects the Journal at the module checkpoints, not every week.
