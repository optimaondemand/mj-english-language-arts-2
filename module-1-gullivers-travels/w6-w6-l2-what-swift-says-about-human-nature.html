💡 THE ONE IDEA TO HOLD ONTO
A theme is the big idea about life that an author builds across a story. To find it, climb from the details to the claim. Here is the climb for Part IV:
STEP 1 · THE DETAILS
Houyhnhnms are honest, calm, and ruled by reason. Yahoos are greedy, violent, and ruled by appetite. Humans, the master decides, look and act like Yahoos.
⬇
STEP 2 · THE PATTERN
The master says humans have “some small pittance of reason” but use it “to aggravate our natural corruptions.” Reason makes us worse, not better.
⬇
STEP 3 · THE THEME (a claim about human nature)
Swift suggests that human pride in our reason is misplaced, because we use that reason to feed our worst appetites rather than to govern them.
A theme is a claim, not a topic. “Human nature” is a topic. “Swift suggests that…” is a theme. Your wording can differ. The climb is the same.

📚 PART 2 · READ & NOTICE: READ CHAPTERS 5 THROUGH 8

Read Chapters 5 through 8 now. Gulliver explains England to his master: its wars, its lawyers, its money, its ministers of state. The master is amazed. He cannot see why creatures with reason would invent war, or why a lawyer would be “bred up… in the art of proving… that white is black, and black is white.” He decides a soldier is just “a Yahoo hired to kill… as many of his own species… as possibly he can.”

Then, in Chapters 7 and 8, the contrast lands. The master watches his own Yahoos fight over “shining stones” (greed for greed’s own sake) and decides human greed is the same thing. Gulliver, meanwhile, falls in love with the Houyhnhnms, whose “two principal virtues” are “friendship and benevolence,” and begins to feel disgust for his own kind.

💡 KEEP THIS IN MIND
Swift is not saying humans are exactly the same as Yahoos. He is saying something sharper: that our reason, the thing we are proudest of, often serves our worst appetites instead of taming them. The shining-stones scene is the key. The Yahoos hoard for no reason at all, and the master sees our greed in it.

In the Week 6 section of your Reading Journal, answer in 2 to 4 sentences: the master horse calls a soldier “a Yahoo hired to kill… as many of his own species… as possibly he can.” What does this judgment show about how Swift views human reason? Point to a detail from Chapters 5 through 8.

📚 PART 4 · PAIR THE DETAIL TO THE IDEA

For each detail below, decide in your head what it shows about human nature. Then flip the card to see one way Swift uses it. You will pull one of these into your Quick Write.

DETAIL · tap to flip
A soldier is “a Yahoo hired to kill… as many of his own species… as possibly he can.”
WHAT IT SHOWS
Swift strips the glory off war. War is not honor; it is one human paying another to kill people who never harmed him.
DETAIL · tap to flip
The Yahoos dig for “shining stones” and hoard them, though the stones are of no use.
WHAT IT SHOWS
The master sees human greed in it. We chase wealth for its own sake, the way the Yahoos chase stones they cannot even use.
DETAIL · tap to flip
Humans have “some small pittance of reason” used “to aggravate our natural corruptions.”
WHAT IT SHOWS
This is the heart of the theme. Reason is meant to make us better, but Swift suggests we use it to make our vices worse.
