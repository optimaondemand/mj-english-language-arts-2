Pronouns: Words That Stand in for Nouns
Lesson
⏱ ~12 min lesson
📝 TODAY'S FOCUS
A pronoun is a word that takes the place of a noun, so you do not have to repeat the same name over and over. Words like he, him, they, and it are pronouns. The noun a pronoun stands for is called its antecedent. Today you will find the pronouns in a sentence from Chapter 7 and trace each one back to its noun.
📚 THIS WEEK'S SENTENCE (from Chapter 7)
“The lord came at night, and he warned Gulliver that the court had charged him.”
A shortened version of Swift’s account of the secret warning Gulliver receives.
⚡ WARM-UP
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
In the sentence above, the word he stands in for a name. Whose name does it replace? (No wrong answer.)
💡 THE ONE RULE TO REMEMBER
Every pronoun should point clearly back to one noun. That noun is its antecedent. If a reader cannot tell which noun the pronoun means, the sentence gets confusing.
Each pronoun reaches back to a noun:
the lord  ⟵  he
Gulliver  ⟵  him
noun (antecedent) ⟵ pronoun
When the antecedent is unclear, watch out:
When the lord met Gulliver, he was afraid.
Who was afraid, the lord or Gulliver? The pronoun he does not point clearly to one noun.

“Bolgolam hated Gulliver, so he signed the charges.”
Which noun does he stand for? Click to check.
He stands for Bolgolam, the one who hated Gulliver and signed the charges. Bolgolam is the antecedent.
“Gulliver found the boat and dragged it to shore.”
Which noun does it stand for? Click to check.
It stands for the boat, the thing Gulliver found and dragged. The boat is the antecedent.
📚 HOW TO FIND A PRONOUN: ASK QUESTIONS ABOUT THE SENTENCE

Find the nouns first. Then look for the short words that stand in for them. Watch how it works on this week’s sentence.

  1. Who or what came at night?  the lord. That noun is the first subject noun.
  2. Who or what warned Gulliver?  he. That word stands for a noun and does the warning, so it is a pronoun, and because it is the subject of "warned" it is a subject pronoun. Stands for which noun?  the lord. That is its antecedent.
  3. Whom did the court charge?  him. That word stands for a noun, so it is a pronoun. Stands for which noun?  Gulliver. That is its antecedent.
Label what you found:
The lord
subject noun came
verb at night, and
conjunction he
subject pronoun warned
verb Gulliver
proper noun that the court charged
verb him.
pronoun

Both pronouns here point to a clear antecedent: he reaches back to the lord, and him reaches back to Gulliver. A reader is never left guessing.
