💡 THE SHAPE OF A SHORT ARGUMENT
An argument is not just an opinion. It is a claim you back up with evidence and explanation, then close. Here is the shape your written component takes, and which Quick Write each part grows from.
PART 1 · CLAIM (from Quick Write 5)
State which voyage best reveals Swift’s view of humanity, and name pride as the idea it exposes. One clear sentence.
PART 2 · EVIDENCE + EXPLANATION, FIRST PIECE (from Quick Write 6)
Give one detail from that voyage. Then explain in your own words how it shows pride. The explanation is the part that does the work.
PART 3 · EVIDENCE + EXPLANATION, SECOND PIECE (from Quick Writes 6 and 7)
Give a second detail. Explain how it adds to your point. Two pieces of evidence are stronger than one.
PART 4 · CONCLUSION (from Quick Write 7)
Restate your point in new words and say what it tells us about Swift’s view of people. Do not just repeat the claim.
📚 READ & NOTICE: A WORKED EXAMPLE

Here is a short argument that uses Lilliput. Notice how each labeled part matches the shape above. You will not copy this voyage or these details. It is a model for the moves, not an answer to borrow.

CLAIM The voyage to Lilliput best reveals Swift’s view of humanity, because it shows how pride makes small things look huge.

EVIDENCE 1 Two Lilliputian nations go to war over which end of an egg to crack first. (Then the explanation:) Swift makes the cause of the war as tiny as the people, so a reader sees how proud quarrels look foolish from the outside.

EVIDENCE 2 The Lilliputians choose officials by who can jump highest on a tightrope. (Then the explanation:) Swift makes leadership a circus trick, which suggests that human pride often rewards show instead of worth.

CONCLUSION By shrinking people, Swift lets us see our own pride at a safe distance, which is why Lilliput exposes his view of humanity most sharply.

See how the evidence is never left to speak for itself. Each detail is followed by a sentence that says what it shows about pride. That sentence is the explanation, and it is what earns credit.

📚 CHECK YOUR OWN THINKING

Read this student sentence, which is meant to be the explanation after a piece of evidence:

“This shows that the Lilliputians are very small and fight a lot.”
💡 WHY THAT SENTENCE IS WEAK
In your notebook, write down why this sentence is weak as an explanation, and one way to fix it. Here is what to look for: the sentence only retells the detail. It never says what the detail reveals about pride or about Swift’s view of people. A stronger version names the idea: “This shows that pride can make people treat a tiny disagreement as worth a war, which is exactly the foolishness Swift wants us to notice.” The fix is to push past what happened and answer the question, what does this show about pride. Your wording will differ. The test is whether your explanation reaches the idea, not just the plot.
⚡ TRY IT · NAME EACH PART OF THE ARGUMENT

Below are sentences from a short argument, out of order. For each one, decide which part of the shape it is: claim, evidence, explanation, or conclusion. Decide in your head first, then choose.

1. “This reveals that the king sees England’s proud boasts as small and even harmful.”
2. “The voyage to Brobdingnag best reveals Swift’s view of humanity, because seeing people as giants exposes how small our pride really is.”
3. “By making Gulliver tiny among giants, Swift lets us feel how foolish human pride looks from above, which is why this voyage shows his view of humanity most clearly.”
4. “The giant king listens to Gulliver describe England, then calls his people the most harmful little creatures he has heard of.”
🧠 NOW BUILD YOUR PLAN

Open your Quick Writes and the GT Final Project — Written page. In your notebook or in the doc, fill in the four parts using your own voyage and your own details:

Claim: pull your one-sentence point from Quick Write 5. Make sure it names a voyage and names pride.
Evidence 1 and its explanation: take your strongest detail from Quick Write 6 and write the sentence that says what it shows about pride.
Evidence 2 and its explanation: add a second detail from Quick Write 6 or 7, then explain it the same way.
Conclusion: use your Quick Write 7 synopsis to restate the point in fresh words.
