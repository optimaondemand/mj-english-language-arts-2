📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
Gulliver washes up in a land where the calm, reasoning rulers are horses and the howling, greedy beasts are creatures that look exactly like him. Swift hands you two societies and dares you to compare them.
You will read Chapters 1 through 4. As you read, you will compare and contrast the two groups Gulliver finds: the Houyhnhnms (the horse-people, governed by reason) and the Yahoos (the brutish creatures driven by appetite).
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
I will be able to describe the Houyhnhnms and the Yahoos using details from Chapters 1 through 4.
2
I will be able to sort traits and behaviors into the right society, and name what each one is like.
3
I will be able to explain why Swift makes the wise rulers horses and the brutes look like people.

💡 THE ONE IDEA TO HOLD ONTO
To compare two things is to find how they are alike. To contrast them is to find how they differ. Swift sets two societies in the same country so the differences leap out. Watch the gap between them.
THE HOUYHNHNMS · THE HORSE-PEOPLE
Calm and reasoning. Their grand maxim is “to cultivate reason, and to be wholly governed by it.” They are clean, honest, and orderly. Their word for themselves means “the perfection of nature.” They have no word for lying.
↔
THE YAHOOS · THE BRUTES
Greedy, filthy, and violent. They fight over food and over shiny stones. Gulliver calls them “the most disagreeable animal” he ever saw. The horror is that they have “a perfect human figure.”
The shock of Part IV is the placement. The rational rulers are animals. The disgusting beasts look human. Swift flips the order on purpose, and that flip is where his point about human nature lives.
💡 ONE STEP FURTHER: THE CENTRAL IDEA EACH SOCIETY BUILDS
Describing how the two societies differ is only the first move. Swift is also building a specific central idea through each one, and those two ideas are not the same claim stated twice. Through the Houyhnhnms, he builds the idea that reason followed completely produces order and honesty without any need for law or lies. Through the Yahoos, he builds the idea that appetite left unchecked by reason produces greed and violence that serve no purpose at all. Neither idea sits still across Chapters 1 through 4. The Houyhnhnm idea grows more demanding, since their reason leaves no room even for a well-meant lie. The Yahoo idea grows more disturbing, since their brutishness turns out to live inside a human body.
CENTRAL IDEA · HOUYHNHNMS
Reason, followed completely, needs no law and produces no lies.
CENTRAL IDEA · YAHOOS
Appetite, left unchecked, turns greedy and violent for no purpose at all.
📚 PART 1 · READ & NOTICE: BEFORE YOU READ
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
Picture a country where horses are the wise rulers and the wild beasts look just like humans. What is your first reaction? (No wrong answer. Just check in with yourself.)
📚 PART 2 · READ & NOTICE: READ CHAPTERS 1 THROUGH 4

Read Chapters 1 through 4 now. Gulliver’s own crew turns pirate and leaves him on a strange shore. He first meets the Yahoos, a herd of hairy, clawed creatures who climb trees and fling filth at him. Then a calm gray horse studies him with “manifest tokens of wonder,” and Gulliver realizes the horses, the Houyhnhnms, are the reasoning rulers here.

As Gulliver learns their language, his Houyhnhnm master makes a discovery that mortifies Gulliver: under his clothes, Gulliver is shaped just like a Yahoo. The master cannot even grasp the idea of lying, because his people have “no word in their language to express lying or falsehood.”

💡 KEEP THIS IN MIND
When you compare and contrast, look for the same kind of detail on both sides. If you note how the Houyhnhnms eat (oats and milk, “with much decency and regularity”), look for how the Yahoos eat (tearing flesh, fighting over it). Matching detail to detail is how a contrast becomes evidence.
⚡ TRY IT · WHICH SOCIETY DOES THIS BELONG TO?

Each detail below comes from Chapters 1 through 4. Decide whether it describes the Houyhnhnms or the Yahoos. Think it through before you choose.

1. They confer with one another “like persons deliberating upon some affair of weight.”
2. They leap into a tree and “discharge their excrements” on Gulliver’s head.
3. They have “no word in their language to express lying or falsehood.”
4. Gulliver discovers, “to my everlasting mortification,” that he is shaped like these creatures.
5. They eat oats and milk in clean vessels, “after a very orderly and cleanly manner.”
📚 PART 4 · READ & NOTICE: WHY THE FLIP?

Swift could have made the wise rulers human and the beasts some new monster. Instead he made the rulers horses and gave the beasts a human shape. That choice is not an accident.

In your Reading Journal, in the Week 6 section, write one sentence: why does Swift make the disgusting Yahoos look exactly like humans? Tie your idea back to the resemblance Gulliver keeps trying to hide.

➡️ WHAT YOU'LL DO NEXT
1
Respond in your Reading Journal
In the Week 6 section of your Reading Journal, answer these three in 2 to 4 sentences each, pointing to a detail from Chapters 1 through 4:
  1. Describe one clear way the Houyhnhnms and the Yahoos are opposites. Give one detail about each side.
  2. Gulliver tries hard to hide that he is shaped like a Yahoo. Why is this so upsetting to him? Use a detail from the reading.
  3. The Houyhnhnms have no word for lying. What does that one missing word tell you about how they live?
💪 BEFORE YOU GO

Keep two lists in your Journal as you read on: what makes the Houyhnhnms admirable, and what makes the Yahoos repulsive. Tomorrow’s lesson asks what Swift is saying about human nature through that gap, so the more detail you gather now, the stronger your evidence will be.
