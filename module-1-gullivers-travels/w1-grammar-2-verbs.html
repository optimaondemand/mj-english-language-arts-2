Grammar 1.02
Verbs: The Words That Do the Work
Lesson ≈12 min
📝 TODAY'S FOCUS
A verb shows an action or a state of being. Today you will find the verbs in a sentence from Gulliver’s Travels, learn the difference between an action verb and a linking verb, and see how a helping verb works with a main verb. You will be able to spot the verb in any sentence you read.
📚 THIS WEEK'S SENTENCE (from Chapter 1)
“I swam as fortune directed me, and was pushed forward by wind and tide.”
⚡ WARM-UP
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
Before the lesson, how sure are you that you can point to the verb in a sentence and say whether it shows an action? (No wrong answer.)
💡 THE ONE IDEA TO REMEMBER
The verb is the engine of the sentence. It tells you what the subject does or what the subject is. There are three jobs a verb can do:
Action verb
Shows something the subject does.
In the sentence: swam, directed, pushed
Linking verb
Shows a state of being. Connects the subject to a word that describes it.
Examples: is, am, are, was, were, seem, become
Helping verb
Teams up with a main verb to form a verb phrase.
In the sentence: was helps pushed to make “was pushed”
📚 HOW TO FIND THE VERB: ASK QUESTIONS ABOUT THE SENTENCE

The surest way to find the verb is to ask questions about the sentence and let the answers point you to it. Start with the subject, then ask what is being said about it. Watch how it works on this week’s sentence.

  1. Who or what is the sentence about?  I. Because I is a pronoun, it is the subject pronoun.
  2. What is being said about I?  I swam. The word swam is the verb, and because it shows something the subject does, it is an action verb (sometimes called the action word).
  3. What else is being said about I?  I was pushed forward. This is a two-word verb phrase: was is the helping verb and pushed is the main verb.
  4. Is there a verb in the middle part too?  Yes. “As fortune directed me”: fortune is doing the directing, so directed is an action verb.
Label what you found:
I
subject pronoun swam
action verb as fortune directed
action verb me, and was
helping verb pushed
main verb forward by wind and tide.

Two reminders: not every verb shows action. A linking verb like was, is, or seemed shows a state of being. And when two or more verbs sit together, the last one is usually the main verb and the ones before it are helping verbs.
