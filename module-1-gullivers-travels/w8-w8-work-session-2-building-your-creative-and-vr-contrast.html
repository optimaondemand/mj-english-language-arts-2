📖 WHAT THIS SESSION IS FOR
Show the idea of pride, do not just say it.
You will plan the creative half of your project. First a visual that contrasts Lilliput and Brobdingnag. Then a VR scene that stages the same scale contrast you already built in Week 1 and Week 4. Then a short reflection that ties it to pride. By the end you will know exactly what to make for the GT Final Project — Creative + VR page. This session is practice. It is not graded.
📋 WHAT YOU NEED OPEN
▶
Your saved Week 1 Tiny World VR scene (Gulliver as a giant among the Lilliputians) and your Week 4 Welcome to Brobdingnag VR scene (Gulliver as a speck among giants).
▶
Your VR headset and whatever you will use for the visual: paper and supplies for a diorama, a drawing or design app for digital art, or paper or a doc for a comic strip.
▶
The GT Final Project — Creative + VR page (open it in Canvas in the Module Wrappers and Project area).
💡 THE CONTRAST AT THE HEART OF YOUR PROJECT
Your creative work shows one idea: when you change Gulliver’s size, you change what pride looks like. Lilliput and Brobdingnag are mirror images. Your visual and your VR scene should make a viewer feel the flip.
LILLIPUT (Week 1 scene)
Gulliver is the giant
Tiny people swarm a huge body. Power looks like it sits with the small. Their proud quarrels look silly from above.
⇆
BROBDINGNAG (Week 4 scene)
Gulliver is the speck
A huge hand could lift him. Now his own proud talk of England sounds tiny and harmful to the giant king.
Same person, opposite size. That flip is your subject. A strong project makes the viewer notice that pride looks foolish from both directions.
📚 STEP 1 · CHOOSE YOUR VISUAL

Pick one of three. Whatever you choose, it must show both worlds side by side so the size flip is obvious.

Option What it is Good if you like to
DioramaA two-sided model: tiny figures on the Lilliput side, towering ones on the Brobdingnag side.build with your hands.
Digital artA split image or two panels showing Gulliver huge, then Gulliver tiny.draw or design on a screen.
Comic stripA short strip: one or two panels in Lilliput, then in Brobdingnag, with a caption about pride.tell a story in panels.
🧠 STEP 2 · STAGE THE VR CONTRAST SCENE

You already built both halves. In Week 1 you staged a tiny world with Gulliver as the giant. In Week 4 you staged Brobdingnag with Gulliver as the speck. Now put them in one scene so a viewer feels the flip. Put on your VR headset, open the virtual environment for this course, and choose any setting you like with open ground.

1
On one side, rebuild your Lilliput staging: a large shape for Gulliver surrounded by tiny objects for the Lilliputians.
2
On the other side, rebuild your Brobdingnag staging: a tiny shape for Gulliver beside towering objects for the giants.
3
Add two labels or sticky notes, one per side. On each, write one sentence about how pride looks in that world.
4
Stand between the two sides so both are in view, then record a short video or take a screenshot that captures the contrast. If you cannot capture inside VR, write a short description of what your scene shows instead.
📚 PLAN YOUR REFLECTION

Your reflection is 200 to 250 words. It should explain what your visual and VR scene show about pride, not just describe what you made.

⚡ TRY IT · IS THIS REFLECTION SENTENCE STRONG OR WEAK?

For each sentence, decide whether it explains an idea about pride (strong), only describes what is in the scene (weak), or sits in between. Decide first, then choose.

1. “Putting Gulliver huge on one side and tiny on the other shows that pride looks just as foolish whether you are looking down or looking up.”
2. “On the left I put small green figures, and on the right I put three tall figures and a big hand.”
3. “I placed Gulliver down low among the giants so he would look powerless, which felt important.”
4. “My two labels say the same proud boast, but in Brobdingnag it sounds tiny, which is how Swift makes human pride look small.”
➡️ WHAT YOU’LL DO NEXT
1
Make the three pieces
Build your visual contrast, stage and capture your VR scene, and write your 200 to 250 word reflection. Submit them on the GT Final Project — Creative + VR page.
2
Run the Submission Checklist
Before you turn anything in, open the Submission Checklist page and confirm every piece of both components is ready.
💪 BEFORE YOU GO

You built both worlds weeks ago. Now you set them side by side so a viewer feels the flip, and you say in your own words what that flip shows about pride. Make the contrast clear, and let your reflection reach the idea, not just the picture.
