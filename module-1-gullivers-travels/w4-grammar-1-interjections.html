Grammar 4.01
Interjections: Words That Show Sudden Feeling
Lesson
⏱ ~12 min lesson
📝 TODAY'S FOCUS
An interjection is a word or short phrase that bursts out to show feeling. Think of the sounds Gulliver might make as a giant cat stalks toward him: Oh! Help! Wow! Today you will learn how interjections work, how they are punctuated, and how to spot them. By the end you will be able to use one well in your own writing.
📚 THIS WEEK'S SENTENCE (Part II, Chapter 1, lightly adapted to show feeling)
“Alas, the huge creature waded after the boat!”
Swift describes the reapers chasing the long-boat. We have added one feeling-word at the front to study how it works.
⚡ WARM-UP
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
Imagine you are tiny Gulliver and a cat the size of an ox turns toward you. What word might burst out of you? (No wrong answer.)
💡 THE ONE RULE TO REMEMBER
AN INTERJECTION
A word that bursts out to show feeling.
It is not connected to the rest of the sentence by grammar. You could lift it out and the sentence would still stand.
Strong feeling → use "!"
Help! A monkey has grabbed me!
The interjection gets its own exclamation point.
Mild feeling → use ","
Well, the boat had the start of him.
A softer interjection is set off with a comma.
📚 HOW TO FIND AN INTERJECTION: ASK QUESTIONS ABOUT THE SENTENCE

The surest way to label each word is to ask questions about the sentence and let the answers point to its job. Watch how it works on this week’s sentence. Find the verb first, then the subject, then test the words around them.

  1. What is being said about the creature?  The creature waded. That is the verb.
  2. Who or what waded?  creature. That answer is the subject noun.
  3. The huge: which one or what kind?  huge tells what kind of creature, so it is an adjective. the is an article adjective.
  4. Does the leftover word, Alas, show feeling, and could you lift it out and still have a full sentence?  Yes to both. So it is an interjection. Notice the comma after it.
Label what you found:
Alas,
interjection the
article adjective huge
adjective creature
subject noun waded
verb after the boat.

Two reminders: an interjection does not connect to the other words by grammar, which is why you can lift it out. And the strength of the feeling decides the punctuation: a comma for mild, an exclamation point for strong.
