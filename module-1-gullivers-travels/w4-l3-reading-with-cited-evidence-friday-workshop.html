📚 PART 1 · READ & NOTICE: NOW READ CHAPTERS 4 AND 5

A tour of the kingdom and a string of close calls

Read Part II, Chapters 4 and 5 now. In Chapter 4, Gulliver describes the giant kingdom: a city of six hundred thousand people, a palace seven miles round, and beggars whose sores and lice he can see in horrifying close-up because of his tiny size. The capital is even named Lorbrulgrud, "Pride of the Universe."

Chapter 5 is a chain of accidents that show how fragile a tiny person is in a giant world. Gulliver is knocked flat by giant hailstones, carried off by a spaniel, nearly snatched by a hovering bird, and treated as a plaything by the maids of honour. The most frightening moment comes when a monkey grabs him, carries him to a rooftop, and feeds him "filthy stuff" before a footman rescues him. Through it all, the giants laugh at him. Notice how often Gulliver is the small, ridiculous figure in someone else’s eyes.

💡 A LINE TO MARK
After the monkey adventure, Gulliver reflects: "how vain an attempt it is for a man to endeavour to do himself honour among those who are out of all degree of equality or comparison with him" (Part II, Ch. 5). Mark this line. It is strong evidence for the module question about pride.
📚 PART 2 · READ & NOTICE: PICK THE BEST EVIDENCE

Strong evidence does more than mention the right topic. It directly proves the claim. For each claim below, decide which quoted line is the best support. Read all the options before you choose, and decide in your head first.
