Adjectives: Words That Describe Nouns
Lesson
📝 TODAY'S FOCUS
⌚ ~12 min
An adjective describes a noun. It tells you what kind, which one, or how many. Today you will find the adjectives in a sentence from Chapter 3 and learn three plain questions that catch them every time. Adjectives are how Swift makes a tiny world feel real and exact.
📚 THIS WEEK'S SENTENCE (from Chapter 3)
“The rope-dancers performed upon a slender white thread.”
A shortened version of Swift’s line about the Lilliputian entertainments.
⚡ WARM-UP
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
Picture the rope the dancers use. Which word in the sentence helps you picture it most? (No wrong answer.)
💡 THE ONE RULE TO REMEMBER
An adjective answers one of three questions about a noun. If a word answers any of these, it is doing an adjective’s job.
🎨
What kind?
slender thread, white thread, sour complexion
👈
Which one?
this stick, the chief ministers
🔢
How many?
three threads, nine sticks, six woodmen
📚 HOW TO FIND AN ADJECTIVE: ASK QUESTIONS ABOUT THE SENTENCE

Find the nouns first. Then, for each noun, ask the three questions. Watch how it works on this week’s sentence.

  1. What is being said about the rope-dancers?  The rope-dancers performed. Performed is the verb.
  2. Who or what performed?  rope-dancers. That noun is the subject noun.
  3. Performed upon what?  thread. That is a noun.
  4. What kind of thread?  slender, and also white. Both answer “what kind,” so both are adjectives describing thread.
  5. Which thread?  a. The little word a points to the noun. The words a, an, and the are article adjectives.
Label what you found:
The rope-dancers
subject noun performed
verb upon a
article adjective slender
adjective white
adjective thread.
noun

A noun can have more than one adjective in a row, the way thread here has both slender and white. Each one still answers “what kind.”
