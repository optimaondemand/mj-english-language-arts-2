📋 HOW TO USE THIS PAGE
Your Module 1 Final Project has two components, and they are submitted on two separate pages in the Module Wrappers and Project area. Work down each list below and check off each item as you confirm it. When every box is checked, you are ready to submit. Read the GT Final Project — Rubric once more so you know what earns full credit.
✏️ COMPONENT 1 · WRITTEN ARGUMENT
Submitted as a Word document on the GT Final Project — Written page.
☐
My claim names which voyage best reveals Swift’s view of humanity and names pride as the idea it exposes.
☐
I give two pieces of evidence from the book, each one a real detail I could point to in the text.
☐
After each piece of evidence I wrote an explanation that says what it shows about pride, in my own words.
☐
My conclusion restates the point in fresh words and says what it tells us about Swift’s view of people.
☐
The doc is saved on OneDrive and submitted on the GT Final Project — Written page.
🎨 COMPONENT 2 · CREATIVE AND VR CONTRAST
Submitted on the GT Final Project — Creative + VR page.
☐
My visual contrast (diorama, digital art, or comic strip) shows Lilliput and Brobdingnag side by side so the size flip is obvious.
☐
My VR scene stages both sides of the scale contrast, with a label on each side, and I captured it as a short video or screenshot. If I could not capture it inside VR, I wrote a short description instead.
☐
My reflection is 200 to 250 words and explains what the contrast shows about pride, not just what I made.
☐
All three pieces are uploaded and submitted on the GT Final Project — Creative + VR page.
✅ TWO LAST CHECKS
☐
I read the GT Final Project — Rubric and checked my work against all of its criteria, including intellectual honesty: every idea is mine or is clearly the book’s.
☐
Both components are submitted by the end of Week 8.
🎉
You have finished Module 1.
You read all the way from Lilliput to the land of the wise horses, and you turned what you noticed into an argument and a scene you built with your own hands. That is real work. Submit with confidence, and carry one idea forward: changing your perspective changes what you see, in others and in yourself.
