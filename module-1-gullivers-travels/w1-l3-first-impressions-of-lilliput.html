Lesson 1.03 · Friday Workshop
First Impressions of Lilliput: What Swift Is Showing Us About Pride
Lesson ≈10 min
📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
You have read two chapters of Lilliput. Step back: what is your first impression of this kingdom, and what is Swift quietly showing you about pride?
This is a lighter workshop day. You will pull Chapters 1 and 2 together, review the week’s words, and shape an opinion you can carry into your writing.
📚 WARM-UP · WORD STUDY REVIEW

Say the meaning of each word to yourself first, using how Swift uses it. Then flip the card to check. Each card has its own Reset.

🃏 TAP TO FLIP
diminutive
“these diminutive mortals” (Ch 1)
Very small. Gulliver calls the Lilliputians diminutive mortals because they are only six inches tall.
🃏 TAP TO FLIP
prodigious
“so prodigious a person” (Ch 2)
Huge, enormous. To the Lilliputians, Gulliver is a prodigious creature, almost a moving mountain.
🃏 TAP TO FLIP
conjecture
“as I conjectured by their habits” (Ch 2)
To guess based on clues. Gulliver conjectures who the priests and lawyers are from the clothes they wear.
🃏 TAP TO FLIP
magnanimous
“a most magnanimous prince” (Ch 2)
Brave and generous in spirit. Gulliver calls the emperor magnanimous for not flinching at the drawn sword. Notice the praise is for a thumb-sized king.
📚 READ & NOTICE: TWO CHAPTERS, ONE PICTURE

In Chapter 1 the Lilliputians capture a giant with ropes, arrows, and a clever machine, then feed him and carry him to their city. In Chapter 2 their rulers gather in full ceremony, hold solemn councils, and build a whole official system around him. Two chapters in, a picture has formed: Lilliput is clever, proud, orderly, and very pleased with itself.

Here is the thing Swift wants you to feel. Everything Lilliput does would look impressive if the people were our size. The engineering, the court, the careful laws are all genuinely skillful. But they are six inches tall. Their pride is real, and their smallness is real, at the same time. Swift puts those two truths side by side so we will ask a quiet question about pride: how much of our own grandness would survive if someone could hold us in one hand?

✏️ READING JOURNAL · WEEK 1
Add this to the Week 1 section of your Reading Journal: Write your honest first impression of the Lilliputians in 2 to 3 sentences. Are they more impressive or more ridiculous, and why? There is no single right answer. A strong response points to a real detail from Chapters 1 or 2 to back up your view.
⚡ THINK IT THROUGH

Swift never says “this is a story about pride.” He shows it. Take each detail below and ask yourself what it reveals about Lilliput’s pride. Then add your best two answers to the Week 1 section of your Reading Journal.

Detail 1. The emperor holds his three-inch sword drawn, ready to defend himself from the chained giant. What does this pose show about his pride?

Detail 2. Officials charge fees to grant licenses to come see Gulliver. What does turning a public order into a fee show about this proud government?

Hint: in both, ask what the grand behavior is really for, and who it is meant to impress.

⚠️ WORTH KNOWING
Your first impression of Lilliput is yours. An AI tool can tell you what most readers think, but it cannot have your reaction or back it with the detail that struck you. In your Quick Write today, write the impression you actually formed, then prove it from the text.
