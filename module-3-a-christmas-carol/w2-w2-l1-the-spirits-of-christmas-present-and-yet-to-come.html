📖 TODAY’S FOCUS
You will read Staves 3 and 4. You will notice what each Spirit shows Scrooge, how Scrooge responds, and what he begins to fear.
🎯 BY THE END OF THIS LESSON YOU WILL BE ABLE TO…
1
Describe what the Ghost of Christmas Present shows Scrooge and explain what Scrooge learns from it.
2
Identify what makes the Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come different from the other two spirits, and explain why Dickens gave it no voice.
3
Point to a detail from Stave 3 or 4 and explain what it reveals about Scrooge’s character at that moment.

📚 PART 1 · READ & NOTICE: BEFORE YOU READ
⚡ QUICK CHECK-IN
The Ghost of Christmas Present says “Come in! and know me better, man!” What do you expect this spirit will be like compared to the Ghost of Christmas Past?
📚 PART 2 · READ & NOTICE: NOW READ STAVES 3 AND 4

In Stave 3, a jolly giant in a green robe fills Scrooge’s room with Christmas abundance and invites him to travel. They watch the Cratchit family celebrate Christmas on almost nothing: a small goose, a pudding “like a speckled cannon-ball,” and Tiny Tim on his stool beside the fire. At the Cratchit table, Bob proposes a toast to Scrooge as “Founder of the Feast.” The family drinks it without warmth. When Scrooge asks if Tiny Tim will live, the Spirit turns his own cruel words back on him: “decrease the surplus population.” At the end of Stave 3, Scrooge notices the Spirit is aging. From its robe emerge two ragged children: Ignorance and Want. The Spirit vanishes; the clock strikes midnight.

In Stave 4, a silent black-hooded phantom appears. It only points. It shows Scrooge businessmen who discuss a dead man’s “cheap funeral” without sorrow. It shows three thieves selling stolen bed-curtains and even the shirt off a corpse. It shows a debtor couple quietly relieved that their creditor is dead. Then it shows the Cratchit home, now hushed: Tiny Tim has died. Finally the Spirit points to a neglected gravestone: EBENEZER SCROOGE.

📅 A CRAFT NOTE
Dickens gave the Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come no voice and no face. It is all darkness and one pointing hand. When you notice a craft choice like this, ask: why did the author make that decision? Silence can carry more dread than any words.
📚 PART 3 · READ & NOTICE: WHAT DID SCROOGE SEE?
⚡ TRY IT
Use what you read to answer each question. Decide in your head first.
1. In Stave 3, after Scrooge asks whether Tiny Tim will live, the Spirit quotes Scrooge’s own earlier words back to him. What effect does this have?
2. The Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come never speaks. What does Dickens show Scrooge instead?
3. In Stave 4, Scrooge watches thieves sell stolen items from a dead man’s home. What does he say when he recognizes the situation?
4. Dickens names the two ragged children hidden in the Ghost of Christmas Present’s robe. What are their names, and why does the Spirit say to beware them?
📚 PART 4 · READ & NOTICE: READING JOURNAL
✏️ READING JOURNAL · WEEK 2
Add this to the Week 2 section of your Reading Journal.
1
In Stave 3, the Ghost of Christmas Present shows Scrooge the Cratchit family’s Christmas. What does their celebration show about what truly matters, and what does Scrooge notice that he had not noticed before?
2
In Stave 4, why does Scrooge fear the Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come more than the others? Point to a detail from the text that shows his fear.
3
What connects the two staves? What is Dickens saying about what happens to a person who lives only for himself?
